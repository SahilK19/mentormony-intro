How to use Vacations??

1. Gain some skills

Four types of companies will visit our campus.

  • Coding Related
  • Core Companies
  • Management Related
    • Consultancy
    • Operational
    • Data analytics(Learn SQL,Excel,Python etc.)

And each requires a different set of skills. So identify which company and which type of company will be the best fit for you and then polish your skills according to your interest. But,A thing common between all type of companies is that most of them will take the Aptitude test, GD, case studies ( some of them), Coding (some of them) and interview. So, prepare for all of the above in this summer and improve your English and boost up your communication skills and confidence for interview and GD ( Visit indiabix.com for aptitude and quant test preparations. Visit interviewbit.com for coding related questions.)

You can also get some skills with the help of MOOCs (Massive Open Online Courses) platform like Udemy, Coursera, edx etc. But don't forget to earn the certificate. Because the certificate will be proof that you have that particular skills.

2.Sample essay for financial aid

I’m a student from India, and keen to specialise in the {course name}. I think it will be beneficial for me {Your purpose. Ex-to get a better job opportunity}. Also, due to not having a job, it's challenging for me to carry the expenses to pay for this course on my own. My family runs a small business, and we have a low income. My parents are fully funding my college expenses which are quite expenses for us. As a responsible son/daughter, I do not want to overburden my parents by taking a loan to pay for my courses. Parents can help us only to a certain extent, and I know the value of money and hurdles and difficulties one has to face in earning them. I want to learn about {your course}. But such circumstances drag me back on my aspiration towards exploring my potential in many aspects that require financial obligation. I will also abide by the code of conduct and the honour code of coursera. I would be much grateful if the coursera empathises my situation and support me financially.

3.Do Internship Training

Although, it's very late to apply at any university/professor for a research internship. But, you can ask your guardians or your any relative if they have any contacts in any company, local PwDs, municipal corporations, PSUs etc. so that you will get an internship or training. Even an internship for 4-weeks also gives a boost to your profile. So, Ask your guardian about it. P.S.- Don't try to get a fake certificate if you have any contacts in any organisation. As teacher/ interviewer asks you what you have done in that internship and if you lie at that point or if you have no idea, then you'll automatically get rejected.

4.Prepare Resume

DO's:

  • Be consistent in format and content
  • Make it easy to read and follow, balancing white space
  • Use consistent spacing, underlining, italics, bold, and capitalization for emphasis
  • List headings (such as Experience) in order of importance
  • Within headings, list information in reverse chronological order (most recent first)
  • Avoid information gaps such as a missing summer
  • Be sure that your formatting will translate properly if converted to a .pdf

DONT'S:

  • Lie in resume
  • Use personal pronouns (such as I)
  • Abbreviate
  • Use a narrative style
  • Number or letter categories
  • Use slang or colloquialisms
  • Include a picture
  • Include age or sex
  • List references
  • Start each line with a date

Resume language should be:

  • Specific rather than general
  • Active rather than passive
  • Written to express not impress
  • Articulate rather than “flowery”
  • Fact-based (quantify and qualify)
  • Written for people who scan quick

Top 5 Resume mistakes:

  • Spelling and grammar errors
  • Missing email and phone information
  • Using passive language instead of “action” word
  • Not well organized, concise, or easy to skim
  • Too long
